Armor Hero

They chose courage. We carry the memory.

This art object commemorates those who left civilian life to defend the freedom of Ukraine and its people. It embodies a warrior-defender, originally developed by 3D artist Zakhar Zakharov based on a concept by Thomas Wievegg. In 2022, Zakhar left to defend his homeland and did not return.

The installation manifests a futuristic guardian in blue and yellow, symbolizing courage, memory, and resilience. The figure stands on an illuminated octagonal base resembling tree roots, expressing connection to family, community, and homeland. Together, they form an axis mundi—an enduring structure that carries memory and the human spirit forward.

The Story Behind the Art

The story of this artwork began with the magic of the Playa.

During my first Burning Man, I brought with me a collage with a photo of my cousin; Zakhar, who in early 2022 followed the call of his heart, left everything behind and went to defend his country. Sadly, he never returned.

On the last day of the festival, something truly extraordinary happened. My travel companion noticed a message in one of the Burning Man chats that said:

"Please, if anyone knows who placed Zakhar's photo in the Temple, reach out."

I immediately knew it was about my cousin. I contacted the message sender, and it turned out to be Zakhar's childhood friend; who hadn't known what had happened to him until he saw that photo in the Temple.

I was deeply moved by how; among 70,000 people, it was he, by chance and yet not by chance; who came across that image and, throughout a chain of coincidences, found me.

In that moment, I realized I wanted Zakhar's story to live on, as a story of love, courage, and remembrance. That's how the idea of creating this art piece was born, a tribute to him and to all defenders who shared his fate.

The image we seek to bring to life is based on a 3D character created by Zakhar himself. This robot has already been to Burning Man with me, printed on the collage with his photo. Back in 2019, when he designed it, he somehow foresaw the future of portraying a strong, brave warrior in blue and yellow, the colors of Ukraine.

Now I feel it is my mission to bring this character into the physical world. As a symbol of resilience, inner strength, and memory. Through it, I wish to tell a story of love for one's homeland, of sacrifice, and of the beauty of human spirit that cannot be defeated.

— Liza Kormilets

Testimonies

Цей хлопець працював на плитковому заводі в цеху, де влітку температура +60° +70°, а взимку +40° +50° за С, і мріяв одного разу покинути це місце. Коли він озвучував свої мрії співробітникам і приятелям із заводу, вони сміялися над ним і вмовляли не витрачати час на всяку дурню. Адже робота на заводі це стабільний дохід і повний соціальний пакет, крім того ще й житло для проживання від заводу.

Але Захар не здавався і був вірний своїй мрії. Іноді на зустрічах з нами (родичами і друзями) він розповідав, що почав освоювати 3D і мріє піти з заводу і влаштуватися в компанію з розробки відеоігор. Згодом він набрався майстерності в 3D і вже запитував нас, як ми думаємо, «чи варто йому йти з заводу?», як зараз пам'ятаю, ми всі дружно відповідали йому: «Звичайно, Захаре! Йди з заводу! У тебе все вийде!».

І ось настав наступний етап його творчої діяльності, вже без походів на завод — у сфері гейм-дева. Так працюючи в одній команді, потім в іншій і так далі, Захару вдалося взяти участь у міжнародних ігрових проектах, потрапити в титри міжнародних ігрових проектів, впевнено стати і йти шляхом здійснення своєї мрії.

Поки одного разу не сталося 24 лютого 2022 року...

This guy worked in the tile factory workshop, where in summer the temperature was +60° +70°C, and in winter +40° +50°C, and he dreamed of leaving this place one day. When he voiced his dreams to colleagues and friends from the factory, they laughed at him and persuaded him not to waste time on nonsense. After all, factory work meant stable income and a full social package, plus housing provided by the factory.

But Zakhar didn't give up and stayed true to his dream. Sometimes at gatherings with us (relatives and friends), he shared that he'd started mastering 3D and dreamed of quitting the factory to join a video game development company. Eventually, he gained proficiency in 3D and began asking us, "Should I leave the factory?" I still remember vividly—we all enthusiastically replied: "Of course, Zakhar! Quit the factory! You'll succeed!"

And so came the next stage of his creative career, already without going to the factory—in game dev. Working in one team, then another, and so on, Zakhar managed to participate in international game projects, get credited in international game projects, confidently become one and pursue fulfilling his dream.

Until February 24, 2022 happened...

— Oleg Kormilets (Cousin)

Equipment List

Item Specs
Solar panel 100 W 12 V 43x20 cm (several may be required, depending on calculations)
Charge controller 12 V 100 A with USB 5 V x2
LiFePo4 battery 100 A 12 V 1200 W (actual 960 W / 14 night hours = 68.5 W/hour, possibly several according to calculations)
Tablet computer Equipped with Bluetooth
JBL Bluetooth speaker 20W RMS Bluetooth speaker 5V 3A (15W) - 2.5 hours of charging from USB
Power and lighting control board Includes time relay, light sensor, temperature sensor, power cut-off relay
Wires, connectors Various wiring and connection components

Perimeter Lighting

8 LED lamps IP68 12V 5W - Total 40W

Platform step lighting LED strip in silicone casing IP68:

  • 1 step length 9.33 m 12 V ~7-9 W/m ≈ 70-90 W
  • 2 steps length 7.46 m 12 V ~7-9 W/m ≈ 56-82 W
  • LED strip power connectors: 4 pcs

Note: LED strip consumption must be reduced. Can be replaced with 20 mA spotlights.

Spotlights on the Model

F12mm LED spotlights on the model (different colors):

  • Total ~20 pcs
  • 12V max 20mA
  • Total 0.4A 5W

There should also be spotlights behind the model - approximately 5 pieces.

Note: The list is preliminary; the parameters and models of equipment must be specified during design and selection.

Diagrams

Graphic materials: Light—circles indicate the locations of spotlights. Audio—possible sound output location from the model. Motion sensor—motion sensor. Control unit, inverter, battery—solar power station, if it can be located inside the model.

Orientation diagram: Working with the podium and outdoor lighting. Front view shows the podium floor plan with the model frame in the middle. Around the perimeter, at the top of the octagonal podium, are metal pipes with lighting (8 pcs).

Option for placing solar panels: Solar panels, controller, and batteries can be placed on the podium of the art object. Two panels could be placed—one behind and one on the podium. This needs to be verified with calculations and the appropriate equipment selected.
